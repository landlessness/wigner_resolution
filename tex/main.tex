\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution in the $(x,p)$ plane is bounded below by the Planck scale. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though the quasi-probability distribution is defined pointwise, its negative values carry physically meaningful information. We identify that the interference scales of Zurek are exactly the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. Inscribed within the state's Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p \ge h/2$, these blobs are bitangent to the cell at every quadrature, deforming continuously across the quadrature angle to maintain constant action $h/2$. Integrating convolutions of the Wigner function across the quadrature quorum yields a non-negative portrait with resolution $\pi \hbar^2/(\Delta x \Delta p)$, sub-Planckian by tradition, Planckian by construction.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. Its negativity is an indicator of non-classicality~\cite{kenfack2004,hudson1974} and a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012}. A non-negative reading admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space, supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, and serves as the boundary between the classical and quantum regimes~\cite{marieisert2012,veitch2012}. This letter develops the non-negative context at the resolution permitted by the state's action capacity.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================

The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ in its Robertson--Schr\"odinger form~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. Following de Gosson, who measures phase-space regions by their symplectic capacity~\cite{degosson2009camel,degossoncharlyne2022}, we work with the covariance-ellipsoid spreads $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, which absorb a factor of 2 into the definition. The phase-space area of the covariance ellipsoid has units of action~\cite{degosson2013qblobs}. We call it the action capacity of the state, and the ellipsoid itself the Heisenberg cell (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

Zurek identified two interference scales set by the state's spreads, $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$~\cite{zurek2001,toscano2006}. Each is the squeezed axis of a quantum blob of action $h/2$, tangent to the Heisenberg cell at a principal axis (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\delta x} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\delta p} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
The squeezed cell $a_{\delta x}$ is squeezed along $x$ to the Zurek scale $\delta x$ and stretched along $p$ to the state's spread $\Delta p$; $a_{\delta p}$ is its polar dual, squeezed along $p$ and stretched along $x$~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The shape change is exactly reciprocal because the action is locked at $h/2$, the symplectic-camel principle of de Gosson~\cite{degosson2009camel}: the camel passes through the eye of the needle only by changing its shape. The scales traditionally labeled sub-Planck are the squeezed axes of exactly Planckian cells of action $\pi\hbar$.

These are two members of a one-parameter family $\{a_\theta\}_{\theta \in [0,\pi)}$ of squeezed cells tangent to the Heisenberg cell, each of fixed action
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2.
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
The semi-axis along $\theta$ matches the spread of the state's rotated quadrature $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$, the marginal measured by homodyne detection at phase $\theta$~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009},
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta}.
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
The fixed action of Eq.~(\ref{eq:squeezed_cell}) forces the perpendicular semi-axis to its reciprocal,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar/\delta_\parallel(\theta).
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
At $\theta = 0$ the family recovers $a_{\delta p}$; at $\theta = \pi/2$, $a_{\delta x}$. The family is the orbit of these two blobs under the symplectic group, parameterized by the homodyne quadrature angle (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}).

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the squeezed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. The convolution of the Wigner function of the state with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere. Convolving a Wigner function with a Gaussian of capacity $\ge h/2$ yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,dragoman2005phasespace,cordero2018}. The kernel $K_\theta$ is the unique blob in the squeezed family at orientation $\theta$, and its action is $h/2$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

The phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution of its own action capacity is the integral of $P_\theta$ over the squeezed family,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
Non-negativity is pointwise. $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function.

$\widetilde W$ varies on scales no smaller than the cell with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$. We call it the quorum cell, after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, of action
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p).
\label{eq:a_inner}
\end{equation}
The scale Zurek identified is $a_Z = a/\pi = \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the same scale recovered here from the symplectic geometry of the state. The quorum cell is the common interior of the squeezed family, sharper than any member, but every member has action $h/2$, the uncertainty floor.

The construction extends to higher dimensions through the symplectic capacity of the full Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance ellipsoid~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DEMONSTRATION
% ===============================================================================

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show the portrait across eight states. With the kernel built from each state's action capacity, the positive peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$ with the same count and location, without negativity. The integral across the squeezed family gives $\widetilde W$, the non-negative phase-space portrait that preserves all structure of the state down to the resolution of the quorum cell.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================

Other paths to a non-negative Wigner function include covariance-matched generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976}, the truncated Wigner approximation~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, the Cahill--Glauber $s$-ordered family~\cite{cahillglauber1969}, Born--Jordan and other Cohen-class distributions with interference damping by symplectic rotation~\cite{cordero2018}, and maximum-likelihood reconstruction with Fock-state truncation~\cite{lvovsky2009}. Each succeeds in its regime of validity. Whether the Wigner resolution in those regimes also follows from action capacity remains an open question.

The uncertainty principle $\sigma_x \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ loosens as the action capacity $A$ grows, the transition from micro to macro-mechanics that Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The de Gosson squeezed cell does not. While $a_\theta = h/2$ across every $A \ge h/2$, its axes tighten reciprocally, $\delta_\parallel \delta_\perp = \hbar$, the squeezed axis $\delta = \hbar/\Delta$ contracting as the conjugate spread $\Delta$ grows. Built from the same quadrature quorum as homodyne tomography, the squeeze reaches the quorum cell $a = \pi\hbar^2/A = \hbar^2/(\Delta x \Delta p)$, the Wigner resolution sharpening as action capacity grows.
