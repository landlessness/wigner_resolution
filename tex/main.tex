\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The Wigner function of a quantum state takes negative values, useful in some contexts, not in others. We convolve the Wigner function with the Gaussian of the de Gosson quantum blob with action $h/2$ inscribed and squeezed in the state's Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi\Delta x\Delta p \ge h/2$ over every quadrature of the state. The result is a non-negative portrait that preserves structure down to the quorum cell $\hbar^2 / (\Delta x \Delta p)$, an ellipse with semi-axes equal to the interference scales of Zurek.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty relations, Symplectic geometry, Squeezed states, Quantum state tomography}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================
The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. Its negativity is an indicator of non-classicality~\cite{kenfack2004} and a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012}. A non-negative reading admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. This letter develops the non-negative context at the resolution permitted by the state's action capacity.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================

The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ in its Robertson--Schr\"odinger form~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. Following de Gosson, we work with the covariance-ellipsoid spreads $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, which absorb a factor of 2 into the definition. The phase-space area of the covariance ellipsoid has units of action~\cite{degosson2009camel,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. We call it the action capacity of the state, and the ellipsoid itself the Heisenberg cell (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

De Gosson proved that the Heisenberg cell contains a quantum blob if and only if the state is physically realizable~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. A quantum blob is a symplectic image of the phase-space ball of radius $\sqrt{\hbar}$, of capacity $\pi\hbar = h/2$, the minimum allowed by the symplectic-camel principle~\cite{degosson2009camel}. For a Heisenberg cell larger than $h/2$ the contained blobs form a family, the orbit of the unit blob under the symplectic group.

Zurek identified an interference scale set by the state's spread orthogonal to a given direction in phase space~\cite{zurek2001,toscano2006}. For each direction $\theta$, that scale is the minor axis of exactly one blob in de Gosson's orbit, the squeezed cell $a_\theta$ tangent to the Heisenberg cell (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}).
The semi-axis along $\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta}.
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
The squeezed cell cannot stretch to reach the Heisenberg cell's boundary without a reciprocal squeeze orthogonal to it. The perpendicular semi-axis is forced by the locked capacity $h/2$ to
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar/\delta_\parallel(\theta).
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}

This is the symplectic-camel principle of de Gosson~\cite{degosson2009camel}. The camel passes through the eye of the needle only by changing its shape, and the shape change is exactly reciprocal because the capacity is locked at $h/2$. The squeezed cell has fixed action
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2.
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
The parallel semi-axis $\delta_\parallel(\theta)$ matches the spread of the state's rotated quadrature $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$, the marginal measured by homodyne detection at phase $\theta$~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}. The family $\{a_\theta\}_{\theta \in [0,\pi)}$ is the one-parameter orbit of squeezed cells inscribed in the Heisenberg cell, parameterized by the homodyne quadrature angle.

Along the principal axes (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), where $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$, the squeezed cells are
\begin{equation}
a_{\delta x} = \pi\,\Delta p\,\delta x = h/2, \qquad a_{\delta p} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
with squeezed axes $\delta x, \delta p$ along the principal directions. The reciprocal relationships $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$ are the interference scales of Zurek~\cite{zurek2001}. The de Gosson squeezed cells and the Zurek scales are two readings of the same action cells. The Heisenberg-cell semi-axes $(\Delta x, \Delta p)$ and the quorum-cell semi-axes $(\delta x, \delta p)$ are linked by symplectic polar duality~\cite{degossoncharlyne2022}. The scales traditionally labeled sub-Planck are the squeezed axes of exactly Planckian cells of action $\pi\hbar$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the squeezed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. The convolution of the Wigner function of the state with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere. Convolving a Wigner function with a Gaussian of capacity $\ge h/2$ yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,cordero2018}. The kernel $K_\theta$ is the unique blob in the squeezed family at orientation $\theta$, and its action is $h/2$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

The phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution of its own action capacity is the integral of $P_\theta$ over the squeezed family,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
Non-negativity is pointwise. $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function.

$\widetilde W$ varies on scales no smaller than the cell with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$. We call it the quorum cell, after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}, of action
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p).
\label{eq:a_inner}
\end{equation}
The scale Zurek identified is $a_Z = a/\pi = \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, the same scale recovered here from the symplectic geometry of the state. The quorum cell is the common interior of the squeezed family, sharper than any member, but every member has action $h/2$, the uncertainty floor.

The construction extends to higher dimensions through the symplectic capacity of the full Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance ellipsoid~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DEMONSTRATION
% ===============================================================================

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show the portrait across eight states. With the kernel built from each state's action capacity, the positive peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$ with the same count and location, without negativity. The integral across the squeezed family gives $\widetilde W$, the non-negative phase-space portrait that preserves the structure of the state down to the resolution of the quorum cell.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================

Other paths to a non-negative Wigner function include covariance-matched generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976}, the truncated Wigner approximation~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, the Cahill--Glauber $s$-ordered family~\cite{cahillglauber1969}, and maximum-likelihood reconstruction with Fock-state truncation~\cite{lvovsky2009}. Each succeeds in its regime of validity. Whether the Wigner resolution in those regimes also follows from action capacity remains an open question.

The uncertainty principle $\sigma_x \sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ loosens as the action capacity $A$ grows, the transition from micro to macro-mechanics that Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The de Gosson squeezed cell does not. While $a_\theta = h/2$ across every $A \ge h/2$, its axes tighten reciprocally, $\delta_\parallel \delta_\perp = \hbar$, the squeezed axis $\delta = \hbar/\Delta$ contracting as the conjugate spread $\Delta$ grows. Built from the same quadrature quorum as homodyne tomography, the squeeze reaches the quorum cell $a = \pi \hbar^2/A = \hbar^2/(\Delta x \Delta p)$, the Wigner resolution sharpening as action capacity grows.
