We work in one degree of freedom and align coordinates with the principal axes of the state's covariance ellipse. The Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance term~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930} vanishes, leaving the position spread $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\sigma_x$ and the momentum spread $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$. The symplectic results we use~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022} extend without modification to higher dimensions and to the full off-diagonal covariance.

The state's covariance defines two phase-space cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). The extended cell, of action capacity
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
is the state's outer extent. Inscribed within it is a one-parameter family of squeezed cells of fixed action capacity
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
identified by de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The semi-axes obey the polar-dual identity
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
The cell's action is fixed at $h/2$ by Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985}. As the orientation $\theta$ varies,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar / \delta_\parallel(\theta),
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
where $\theta$ is the rotated quadrature direction of continuous-variable tomography~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}. The minor axis is considered squeezed and the major axis anti-squeezed~\cite{teichsaleh1989}. At the principal-axis orientations $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), the minor axes reduce to the interference scales identified by Zurek~\cite{zurek2001},
\begin{equation}
\delta x = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta p}, \qquad \delta p = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta x}.
\label{eq:zurek}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}

The kernel
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$, has action $a_\theta = h/2$. The state seen at this resolution is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
This is non-negative everywhere. The convolution of two Wigner functions equals the trace inner product of the corresponding density operators~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}, which is non-negative for any pair of states. The non-negativity is a standard fact of the de Gosson and quantum-optics literature. What is new is the kernel's uniqueness. Its covariance is fixed by the state, its action is fixed at $h/2$, and its orientation $\theta$ ranges over the inscribed family.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE ROTATION-AVERAGED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

Averaging $P_\theta$ over the inscribed family removes the orientation parameter and returns a single non-negative density,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta = (W * \bar{K})(x,p),
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
with rotation-averaged kernel $\bar{K}(x,p) = (1/\pi)\int_0^\pi K_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta$. Non-negativity is pointwise: $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function. No marginal is taken; $\widetilde{W}$ is a 2D density on the same domain as $W$. The inner envelope of $\widetilde{W}$ is the Zurek cell with semi-axes $\delta x, \delta p$ and action
\begin{equation}
a_Z = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p},
\label{eq:aZ}
\end{equation}
the polar dual of the extended cell:
\begin{equation}
A \cdot a_Z = a_\theta^{\,2} = (h/2)^2.
\label{eq:polarDualActions}
\end{equation}
The three actions form a geometric progression around the quantum of action with common ratio $\nu = \Delta x\,\Delta p / \hbar$,
\begin{equation}
A : a_\theta : a_Z = \nu : 1 : 1/\nu \quad \text{in units of } h/2.
\label{eq:actionLadder}
\end{equation}
The Zurek-cell scale is an ensemble resolution: it is reached by querying copies of the state at every $\theta$, as in continuous-variable tomography. The single-copy floor is the extended-cell action $A$.
