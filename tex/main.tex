The state's covariance defines two phase-space cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). The extended cell is the state's outer extent in phase space. Its action capacity is
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
where the lower bound is the uncertainty principle. Inscribed within the extended cell is a one-parameter family of squeezed cells. Each carries the quantum of action,
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2,
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
the family identified by de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The two semi-axes are reciprocal in $\hbar$, saturating the uncertainty relation,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
As $\theta$ varies, the inscribed cell rotates within the extended cell and changes aspect ratio. Its area is preserved. The two semi-axes trade off reciprocally,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar / \delta_\parallel(\theta),
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
The conservation of cell area under rotation is Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985}, the uncertainty principle in its symplectic form. At the principal-axis orientations $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), the minor axes reduce to the Zurek interference scales~\cite{zurek2001},
\begin{equation}
\delta x = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta p}, \qquad \delta p = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta x},
\label{eq:zurek}
\end{equation}
which are the de Gosson action identity $a_\theta = h/2$ rewritten as a scale. The action identity $\pi\,\Delta p\,\delta x = h/2$ is identical to $\delta x = \hbar / \Delta p$, and $\pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2$ is identical to $\delta p = \hbar / \Delta x$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}

The Wigner function of the inscribed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. Its action is $a_\theta = h/2$. The state at the resolution of the inscribed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the convolution
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
The value of $P_\theta$ at any point in phase space is the probability that the state passes a test prepared in the inscribed cell centered at that point. Probabilities are non-negative, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere. Formally, the convolution of two Wigner functions equals the trace inner product $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho_1\rho_2)$ of the corresponding density operators~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}, which is non-negative for any pair of states. The non-negativity is a standard fact of the de Gosson and quantum-optics literature. What is new is the kernel's uniqueness. Its covariance is fixed by the state, its action is fixed at $h/2$, and its orientation $\theta$ ranges over the inscribed family.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

$\widetilde W$ is the action-capacity portrait of the state, given by the integral of $P_\theta$ inside $A$,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta = (W * \bar{K})(x,p),
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
with integrated kernel $\bar{K}(x,p) = (1/\pi)\int_0^\pi K_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta$. Non-negativity is pointwise. $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function.

$\widetilde W$ varies on scales with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$. This inner cell $a$ shares the aspect ratio of the extended cell $A$, with semi-axes reciprocal in $\hbar$. Its action is
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p).
\label{eq:a_inner}
\end{equation}
The outer and inner cell actions are reciprocal across the squeezed-cell floor,
\begin{equation}
A \cdot a = a_\theta^{\,2} = (h/2)^2.
\label{eq:reciprocalActions}
\end{equation}
Letting $N = 2A/h$ count the squeezed cells the extended cell holds,
\begin{equation}
A : a_\theta : a = N : 1 : N^{-1} \quad \text{in units of } h/2.
\label{eq:actionLadder}
\end{equation}
No single $K_\theta$ attains the resolution $a$. Only the integral over the squeezed-cell family does. The scale Zurek identified is $a_Z = a/\pi = \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$.

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show the construction across eight states. With the kernel set from each state's covariance, the same number of positive peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$, without negativity. Integrating across the inscribed family delivers $\widetilde{W}$, the non-negative 2D portrait whose resolution floor is the Zurek cell.

The construction takes its simplest form in one degree of freedom with axis-aligned coordinates, used throughout the body. It extends to higher dimensions and to the full covariance matrix including the $x$-$p$ correlation~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}; the general case is treated in the supplement.
