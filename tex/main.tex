\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy\orcidlink{0000-0002-1803-3172}}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher, Corktown, Detroit, Michigan, USA}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Phase-space formulations of quantum mechanics usually ignore that resolution is bounded below by Planck's quantum of action. In the Wigner formulation, the justification is that even though pointwise resolution forces the quasi-probability negative, those negative values carry physical structure. Zurek located that structure at scales set by the state's action capacity. We identify those scales as exactly the axes of de Gosson quantum blobs. Inscribed within the state's elliptical Heisenberg cell of action capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p$, these blobs form a family bitangent to the cell, deforming at constant action $h/2$. To enforce Planck's boundary from below, integrating convolutions of the Wigner function with this $h/2$ family yields a non-negative portrait with sub-Planck resolution $\pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$, sharpening with capacity.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics, Wigner functions, Uncertainty principle, Symplectic geometry, Schrödinger cat states}

The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. It is defined pointwise, below Planck's quantum of action, and Hudson's theorem forces it negative there~\cite{hudson1974}. Its negativity is a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012} and has been characterized by several quasi-probability measures~\cite{kenfack2004}. A non-negative portrait admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci} and supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}. This letter develops the non-negative portrait at the resolution permitted by the state's action capacity.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================
The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. Symplectic capacity measures phase-space regions following de Gosson~\cite{degosson2009camel}. The state's covariance ellipsoid, with semi-axes $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$ and $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, has symplectic capacity $\pi \Delta x \Delta p$, the action capacity of the state. The ellipsoid is its Heisenberg cell (Figs.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs},~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

% ===============================================================================
%  ZUREK SCALES AS A FAMILY OF DE GOSSON BLOBS
% ===============================================================================
Wigner negativity carries physical structure at small scales. Zurek located these scales at $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$, set by the state's action capacity~\cite{zurek2001}. We identify these as the principal axes of a family of de Gosson quantum blobs. These blobs are inscribed within the Heisenberg cell and bitangent to it (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_{\theta=0} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2, \qquad a_{\pi/2} = \pi\,\delta x\,\Delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Zurek's relations give all four semi-axes exactly. The capacities $\Delta x, \Delta p$ run along the major axes, the resolutions $\delta x, \delta p$ along the minor. Capacity and resolution are reciprocal at action $h/2$, the symplectic-camel principle of de Gosson~\cite{degosson2009camel,degosson2013qblobs}. The camel passes through the eye of the needle only by changing its shape.

The principal blobs at $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ are two members of a family that deforms across the quadrature angle, the orientation of the bitangent blob within the Heisenberg cell. At each $\theta \in [0,\pi)$, a blob $a_\theta$ bitangent to the Heisenberg cell has semi-axes
\begin{equation}
r_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta}, \qquad r_\perp(\theta) = \hbar/r_\parallel(\theta).
\label{eq:bitangent_axes}
\end{equation}
The parallel semi-axis $r_\parallel(\theta)$ runs along the quadrature direction $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$, the same quadrature parameterized by homodyne detection~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}. The action is $h/2$ across every $\theta$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:bitangent_blobs}),
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,r_\parallel(\theta)\,r_\perp(\theta) = h/2.
\label{eq:bitangent_blob}
\end{equation}


% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the bitangent blob at quadrature angle $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{r_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{r_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Ktheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. The convolution of the Wigner function of the state with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
The bitangent kernel $K_\theta$ at quadrature angle $\theta$ has action $h/2$. Pointwise resolution forces $W$ negative~\cite{hudson1974}. Convolving with a Gaussian of action $\ge h/2$ lifts the resolution to Planck's floor and yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976,degosson2005bullsci,dragoman2005phasespace,cordero2018}, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================
Integrating $P_\theta$ over the bitangent family gives the phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution set by its action capacity,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function. $\widetilde W \ge 0$ everywhere. Tomographic reconstruction inverts marginals to recover $W$ pointwise~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}; the construction here convolves $W$ with $K_\theta$ and integrates over $\theta$.

$\widetilde W$ has structure at the characteristic scale of the quorum cell, named after the quorum of observables in optical tomography~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001}. The quorum cell is the common interior of the deforming bitangent family, an ellipse with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$ and resolution $\tilde a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_blobs}), equivalently
\begin{equation}
\tilde a = \frac{\pi\hbar^2}{\Delta x\,\Delta p}.
\label{eq:tildea}
\end{equation}
The structures are visible in column 5 of Figs.~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat}. The Heisenberg cell and the quorum cell are reciprocal at the Planck quantum, $A\,\tilde a = (h/2)^2$.

Without loss of generality, the treatment here addresses one spatial dimension with covariance ellipsoid aligned to principal axes. The construction extends to rotated ellipsoids through the symplectic covariance of the bitangent family~\cite{cordero2018}, and to higher dimensions through the symplectic capacity bounded by the Robertson--Schr\"odinger inequality~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs},
\begin{equation}
\sigma_x^2 \sigma_p^2 - \mathrm{Cov}(x,p)^2 \ge \left(\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)^2.
\label{eq:rs}
\end{equation}

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show numerical results across eight states. $\widetilde W$ is non-negative across all eight. By inspection, the positive lobes of $W$ (column 1) appear in $\widetilde W$ (column 5) with the same count and location at the resolution of the quorum cell $\tilde{a}$.

% ===============================================================================
%  DISCUSSION
% ===============================================================================

The uncertainty product $\sigma_x \sigma_p$ grows unbounded, the micro-to-macro transition Heisenberg~\cite{heisenberg1927} and Bohr~\cite{bohr1928} identified as requiring special care. The bitangent blob does not. Its action is $h/2$ across every $A \ge h/2$, and its resolution sharpens with capacity.

Squeezing of experimental practice~\cite{walls1983} saturates the uncertainty product, $\sigma_x \sigma_p = \hbar/2$. This is action capacity feeding conjugate resolution, $\delta = \hbar/\Delta$. Capacity along one axis sets resolution along the other, $\Delta x$ to $\delta p$ and $\Delta p$ to $\delta x$.

A Schr\"odinger cat with $\Delta x \sim 10$ cm and $\Delta p \sim 1$ kg$\cdot$m/s has positional resolution $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p \sim 10^{-34}$ m, twenty-four orders of magnitude below the size of the single radioactive atom that triggers its apparatus~\cite{schrodinger1935trimmer}. The correspondence principle follows from $A \to \infty$, the physical limit underlying the heuristic $h \to 0$.

Other paths to a non-negative Wigner function include covariance-matched generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,hudson1974,cartwright1976}, the truncated Wigner approximation~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, and the Cahill--Glauber $s$-ordered family~\cite{cahillglauber1969}. Each is non-negative at scales set by $\Delta$ or $\delta$, with the relations coincident at $A \sim h/2$. Whether quantum blob deformation underlies these scales more broadly, beyond $A \sim h/2$, is an open question.
