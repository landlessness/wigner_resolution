We work in one degree of freedom and align coordinates with the principal axes of the state's covariance ellipse. The Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance term~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930} vanishes, leaving the position spread $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\sigma_x$ and the momentum spread $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\sigma_p$. The symplectic results we use~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022} extend without modification to higher dimensions and to the full off-diagonal covariance.

The state's covariance defines two phase-space cells (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). The extended cell, of action
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
is the state's outer extent, floored at the quantum of action by Heisenberg. Inscribed within it is a one-parameter family of squeezed cells of fixed action
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta_\parallel\,\delta_\perp = h/2
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
identified by de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The semi-axes obey the polar-dual identity
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel\,\delta_\perp = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
The minor axis is squeezed below the coherent-state scale and the major axis is anti-squeezed above it. Their product is fixed at one quantum of action by Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985}. As the orientation $\theta$ varies,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar / \delta_\parallel(\theta),
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
where $\theta$ is the rotated quadrature direction of continuous-variable tomography~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}. At the principal-axis orientations $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), the minor axes reduce to the sub-Planck scales identified by Zurek~\cite{zurek2001},
\begin{equation}
\delta x = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta p}, \qquad \delta p = \frac{\hbar}{\Delta x}.
\label{eq:zurek}
\end{equation}
The smallest cell the state can locally carry, the spacing of the peaks in its Wigner function, and the boundary at which $W$ changes sign-character are all the same length, set by the action of the state.
