\title{Wigner Resolution from Action Capacity}

\author{Brian S. Mulloy}
\email{bmulloy@umich.edu}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The Wigner function of a quantum state takes negative values, useful in some contexts and not in others. We convolve the Wigner function with the Gaussian of the de Gosson quantum blob $a_\theta = h/2$ inscribed and squeezed in the state's extended cell of action capacity $A = \pi\Delta x\Delta p \ge h/2$ and integrate over $\theta$. The result is a non-negative phase-space portrait that preserves all structure down to the quorum cell $a = \pi \delta x \delta p \le h/2$, an ellipse with semi-axes equal to the sub-Planck scales of Zurek.
\end{abstract}

% ===============================================================================
%  OPENING
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function represents a quantum state in phase space~\cite{wigner1932,hillery1984}. Its negativity is an indicator of non-classicality~\cite{kenfack2004,hudson1974} and a resource for quantum advantage~\cite{veitch2012,marieisert2012}. A non-negative reading admits a literal probability interpretation in phase space, supports classical sampling of quantum dynamics~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, and serves as the boundary between the classical and quantum regimes~\cite{marieisert2012,veitch2012}. This letter develops the non-negative context at the resolution permitted by the state's action capacity.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE PHASE-SPACE CELLS
% ===============================================================================

The state is constrained by the uncertainty principle $\sigma_x\,\sigma_p \ge \hbar/2$ in its Robertson--Schr\"odinger form~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930}, where $\sigma_x, \sigma_p$ are the standard deviations of position and momentum. Following de Gosson, we work with the covariance-ellipsoid spreads $\Delta x = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_x$, $\Delta p = \sqrt{2}\,\sigma_p$, which absorb a factor of 2 into the definition. The covariance ellipsoid of the state has symplectic capacity with units of action~\cite{degosson2009camel,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}; we call this the action capacity of the state, and the ellipsoid itself the extended cell (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}):
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2.
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}

De Gosson proved that the extended cell contains a quantum blob if and only if the state is physically realizable~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. A quantum blob is a symplectic image of the phase-space ball of radius $\sqrt{\hbar}$, of capacity $\pi\hbar = h/2$, the minimum allowed by Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985,degosson2009camel}. For an extended cell larger than $h/2$ the contained blobs form a family, the orbit of the unit blob under the symplectic group.

Among the contained blobs, the one whose semi-axis along direction $\theta$ equals the radius of the extended cell along $\theta$ is the squeezed cell $a_\theta$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). Its semi-axis along $\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
the radius of the extended cell along $\theta$. The squeezed cell cannot reach the boundary of the extended cell along $\theta$ without paying for the stretch with a reciprocal squeeze orthogonal to it. The perpendicular semi-axis is forced by the locked capacity $h/2$ to
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar/\delta_\parallel(\theta).
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
This is the symplectic-camel principle of de Gosson~\cite{degosson2009camel,gromov1985}: the camel passes through the eye of the needle only by changing its shape, and the shape change is exactly reciprocal because the capacity is locked at $h/2$. The squeezed cell has fixed action
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2.
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
The shorter of the two semi-axes is the squeezed axis of $a_\theta$, $\min(\delta_\parallel(\theta), \delta_\perp(\theta))$, and the longer is the anti-squeezed axis, $\max(\delta_\parallel(\theta), \delta_\perp(\theta))$. The semi-axis $\delta_\parallel(\theta)$ is the spread of the state's rotated quadrature $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$, the marginal measured by homodyne detection at phase $\theta$~\cite{vogelrisken1989,lvovsky2009}; $\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar/\delta_\parallel(\theta)$ is the uncertainty-saturated spread of the conjugate quadrature. The family $\{a_\theta\}_{\theta \in [0,\pi)}$ is the one-parameter orbit of squeezed cells inscribed in the extended cell, parameterized by the quadrature angle of optical tomography.

Along the principal axes (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}), where $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$, the squeezed cells are
\begin{equation}
a_{\delta x} = \pi\,\Delta p\,\delta x = h/2, \qquad a_{\delta p} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2,
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
with squeezed axes $\delta x, \delta p$ and anti-squeezed axes $\Delta p, \Delta x$. The reciprocal relationships $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$ are the sub-Planck scales identified by Zurek~\cite{zurek2001}. The de Gosson squeezed cells and the Zurek scales are two readings of the same action cells. The scales traditionally labeled sub-Planck are the squeezed axes of exactly Planckian cells of action $\pi\hbar$.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the squeezed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. The convolution of the Wigner function of the state with $K_\theta$ is
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
$P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere; convolving a Wigner function with a Gaussian of capacity $\ge h/2$ yields a non-negative function~\cite{cartwright1976}.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

The phase-space portrait of the state at the resolution of its own action capacity is the integral of $P_\theta$ over the squeezed family,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta.
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
Non-negativity is pointwise. $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function.

$\widetilde W$ varies on scales no smaller than the cell with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$. This is the quorum cell~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001} $a$, of action
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p).
\label{eq:a_inner}
\end{equation}
The scale Zurek identified is $a_Z = a/\pi = \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$. The quorum cell is the geometric common interior of the squeezed family, the region accessible to every member. Its area is below $h/2$ but resolves the quorum of $a_\theta = \pi\hbar$ cells across orientations.

The phase-space cells, the squeezed family, and the convolved portrait extend without modification to higher dimensions and to states with $x$-$p$ correlations, where the extended cell is the full Robertson--Schr\"odinger covariance ellipsoid~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}.

% ===============================================================================
%  DEMONSTRATION
% ===============================================================================

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show the portrait across eight states. With the kernel built from each state's action capacity, the positive peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$ with the same count and location, without negativity. The integral across the squeezed family gives $\widetilde W$, the non-negative phase-space portrait at the state's action-capacity resolution.

Other paths to a non-negative Wigner function --- covariance-matched generalized Husimi smoothing~\cite{husimi1940,cartwright1976}, the truncated Wigner approximation~\cite{polkovnikov2010}, the Cahill--Glauber $s$-ordered family~\cite{cahillglauber1969}, maximum-likelihood reconstruction with Fock-state truncation~\cite{lvovsky2009} --- succeed in their regimes of validity and fail outside them. In their regimes of success they smooth at the action capacity. The supplement shows the four cases. The action capacity is the Wigner function's resolution floor, and the de Gosson squeezed cell is its physical signature.
