The state's action capacity is
\begin{equation}
A = \pi\,\Delta x\,\Delta p \ge h/2,
\label{eq:extended_cell}
\end{equation}
with the lower bound set by the uncertainty principle. $A$ is the area of the extended cell, the state's outer extent in phase space (Fig.~\ref{fig:de_gosson_cells}). Inside the extended cell sits a one-parameter family of squeezed cells, each of fixed action
\begin{equation}
a_\theta = \pi\,\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = h/2,
\label{eq:squeezed_cell}
\end{equation}
the family identified by de Gosson~\cite{degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}. The two semi-axes are reciprocal in $\hbar$, saturating the uncertainty relation,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta)\,\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar.
\label{eq:polar_dual}
\end{equation}
As $\theta$ varies, the squeezed cell rotates within the extended cell and changes aspect ratio. Its area is preserved. The two semi-axes trade off reciprocally,
\begin{equation}
\delta_\parallel(\theta) = \sqrt{\Delta x^2\cos^2\theta + \Delta p^2\sin^2\theta},
\label{eq:delta_parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\delta_\perp(\theta) = \hbar / \delta_\parallel(\theta).
\label{eq:delta_perp}
\end{equation}
The conservation of cell area under rotation is Gromov's non-squeezing theorem~\cite{gromov1985}. The squeezed cells along the principal axes (Fig.~\ref{fig:zurek_cells}) are
\begin{equation}
a_{\delta x} = \pi\,\Delta p\,\delta x = h/2, \qquad a_{\delta p} = \pi\,\Delta x\,\delta p = h/2.
\label{eq:principal_axis_cells}
\end{equation}
Each cell's minor axis is the corresponding Zurek interference scale~\cite{zurek2001}, $\delta x = \hbar/\Delta p$ and $\delta p = \hbar/\Delta x$. The de Gosson areas and the Zurek scales are two readings of the same action cells.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE BOUNDARY AND THE KERNEL
% ===============================================================================

The Wigner function of the state is
\begin{equation}
W(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\int \psi^*(x+s)\,\psi(x-s)\,e^{2ips/\hbar}\,ds.
\label{eq:wigner}
\end{equation}
The Wigner function of the squeezed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the Gaussian
\begin{equation}
K_\theta(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}\exp\!\left(-\frac{x_\theta^2}{\delta_\parallel(\theta)^2} - \frac{p_\theta^2}{\delta_\perp(\theta)^2}\right),
\label{eq:Gtheta}
\end{equation}
with $x_\theta = x\cos\theta + p\sin\theta$ and $p_\theta = -x\sin\theta + p\cos\theta$. Its action is $a_\theta = h/2$. The state at the resolution of the squeezed cell at orientation $\theta$ is the convolution
\begin{equation}
P_\theta(x,p) = (W * K_\theta)(x,p).
\label{eq:Ptheta}
\end{equation}
The value of $P_\theta$ at any point in phase space is the probability that the state passes a test prepared in the squeezed cell centered at that point. Probabilities are non-negative, so $P_\theta$ is non-negative everywhere. The convolution of two Wigner functions equals the trace inner product $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho_1\rho_2)$ of the corresponding density operators~\cite{degosson2005bullsci,degosson2013qblobs}. The kernel $K_\theta$ is unique. Its covariance is fixed by the state, its action is fixed at $h/2$, and its orientation $\theta$ ranges over the squeezed family.

% ===============================================================================
%  THE CONVOLVED PORTRAIT
% ===============================================================================

$\widetilde W$ is the integral of $P_\theta$ over the squeezed family,
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{W}(x,p) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi P_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta = (W * \bar{K})(x,p),
\label{eq:tildeW}
\end{equation}
with integrated kernel $\bar{K}(x,p) = (1/\pi)\int_0^\pi K_\theta(x,p)\,d\theta$. Non-negativity is pointwise. $P_\theta(x,p) \ge 0$ for every $\theta$, and a non-negative integrand integrates to a non-negative function.

$\widetilde W$ varies on scales no smaller than the cell with semi-axes $\delta x$ and $\delta p$. This is the quorum cell~\cite{darianomacconeparis2001} $a$. It shares the aspect ratio of the extended cell, with semi-axes reciprocal in $\hbar$. Its action is
\begin{equation}
a = \pi\,\delta x\,\delta p = \pi\hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p).
\label{eq:a_inner}
\end{equation}
The action capacity and the quorum cell action are reciprocal across the squeezed-cell floor,
\begin{equation}
A \cdot a = a_\theta^{\,2} = (h/2)^2.
\label{eq:reciprocalActions}
\end{equation}
$N = 2A/h$ counts the squeezed cells the extended cell holds. The three actions sit in the ratio
\begin{equation}
A : a_\theta : a = N : 1 : N^{-1} \quad \text{in units of } h/2.
\label{eq:actionLadder}
\end{equation}
No single $K_\theta$ has resolution $a$. Only the integral over the squeezed family does. The scale Zurek identified is $a_Z = a/\pi = \hbar^2/(\Delta x\,\Delta p)$.

Figures~\ref{fig:eigen} and~\ref{fig:cat} show the construction across eight states. With the kernel built from each state's spreads $\Delta x$ and $\Delta p$, the same number of positive peaks of $W$ along the chosen cross-section appear in $P_{\pi/2}$, without negativity. Integrating across the squeezed family delivers $\widetilde W$, the non-negative phase-space portrait whose resolution floor is the quorum cell.

The construction is written in one degree of freedom with axis-aligned coordinates. It extends to higher dimensions and to the full covariance matrix including the $x$-$p$ correlation~\cite{robertson1929,schrodinger1930,degosson2013qblobs,degossoncharlyne2022}; the general case is in the supplement.
